Margaret Y. Mansaray

Manager

Finalist of the Africa Prize for Engineering Innovation (2023), founder and CEO of Women in Energy (2020), Margaret Yainkain Mansaray is a visionary Sierra Leonean who imagined and brought the Smart Green Stove (STS).

Finalist of the Africa Prize for Engineering Innovation (2023), founder and CEO of Women in Energy (2020), Margaret Yainkain Mansaray is a visionary Sierra Leonean who imagined and brought the Smart Green Stove (STS). Concerned about the fate of women and girls who often go through dangerous conditions to take care of their homes and more particularly the risks they face when fetching wood for cooking (assaults, accidents, etc.). The SGS is made of affordable materials including: coconut fiber, sawdust, recycled frame, etc. Which reduces fuel consumption by 70% according to ‘the Benchmarker’. “I started with a vision to create an accessible, affordable and reliable source of green energy for women who were most vulnerable to energy poverty.” She says to Forbes Africa. Her clean cooking solution has convinced more than one. She is recognized as a pioneer promoter of gender equality, with Women In Energy (WIE) she encourages women to invest and develop their potential. Her profile is captivating, she is often invited to share her vision and her journey in forums such as: Sierra Leone Girl Submit, Africa women innovations and entrepreneurship Forum (AWIEF) and recently the Youth Connekt Summit 2024.

Emmanuel Lavalie

Speaker

Founder and executive director at Imperi Foundation for Education and Agriculture (IFEA) and founder and CEO of Agriculture Multimedia and Entertainment for Growth, Emmanuel Lavalie is part of the vibrant Sierra Leonean youth.

Founder and executive director at Imperi Foundation for Education and Agriculture (IFEA) and founder and CEO of Agriculture Multimedia and Entertainment for Growth, Emmanuel Lavalie is part of the vibrant Sierra Leonean youth. Creative and eager to improve conditions in his community, he is committed to providing opportunities to young people like him who have talent and willpower. Both organizations (IFEA and AMEG) focus on equipping Sierra Leonean youth with technical skills across diverse fields, including technology, agriculture, education, food production, climate change, community development, and the creative industry. Endowed with leadership and unfailing motivation, he never stops imagining projects aimed at erasing urgent and current problems. Michael Williams

Speaker

Sierra Leone has among its brilliant young people Michael Williams a climate innovator and socio-entrepreneur founder and CEO of “Eco homes recycling Limited”.

Sierra Leone has among its brilliant young people Michael Williams a climate innovator and socio-entrepreneur founder and CEO of “Eco homes recycling Limited”. It focuses on the problem of waste and recycling waste to transform them into bricks (eco-bricks) which will allow for more affordable low-carbon housing buildings for Sierra Leoneans (eco-homes). At a time when the climate and the preservation of the environment are at the center of discussions, it is crucial to have projects like this one that aim to protect the ecosystem and the community. This by gradually educating about the preservation of the planet but also by offering young people training that will give them practical skills thus fighting against unemployment. “Eco homes Recycling limited” educates and trains young people, but also provides solutions against waste and finally fights against the high cost of housing. If you want to learn more then what are you waiting for? Register now for the opportunity to meet Michael Williams and learn more about his creative ideas and most importantly enjoy the entire Youth Empowerment Program!
